Domain 1: Acceptability & Culture

1.1 Will using the App be likely to maintain or increase good health-related behaviours for the expected users irrespective of their cultural identity?

1.2 Is the purpose of the App clear and aligned in a positive manner to the intended userâ€™s health maintenance or improvement, whether directly (end user) or indirectly (via a clinician or service provider using the App on behalf of a user)?

1.3 Does the App make a claim about being intended for, or likely to have significant aspects of cultural sensitivity for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples, specific CALD or CARM communities, LGBTQIA+ people or specific demographic groups? And / or does the App make a claim about taking a trauma-informed approach, being disability affirming or relevant for a specific group or community of people with disabilities such as neurodivergent people?

1.4 Does the app cater for the needs of users who live in regional, rural or remote areas of Australia?

1.5 Does the App provide a means to restrict access to content that could be perceived as sensitive, objectionable or harmful to viewing by a minor or the non-intended recipient?

Domain 2: Safety & Trustworthiness

2.1 Are all restrictions, contraindications, limitations and risks clearly communicated to the intended users including e.g., age, requirement for internet access, time commitments or durations, user qualifications or other restrictions, including situations where the App may not be suitable or recommended?

2.2 Is there any evidence that the App conforms to healthcare safety-by-design principles in relation to the design and operation of the App?

2.3 Where the App purports to provide a specific health benefit or make a specific health claim, does the developer offer any evidence from authoritative sources such as certificates of registration, published peer reviewed references, relevant public health authorities, recommendations from scientifically conducted medical trials, or scientific literature to support those claims?

2.4 If health information is provided by the App, is the health information displayed in the App current?

If the App is not already part of a formal human ethics evaluation process (which will have a formal ethics review process) is there any evidence of compliance with an industry or government ethical code of practice?

2.6 Does the App claim safe operation?

2.7 Does the App clearly identify all costs, temporary or on-going, whether they are for paywalled content or if they are part of subscription services?

Does the App, or associated website, transparently disclose authorship, affiliations, credentials, commercial interests (investors and shareholders), conflicts of interest, and disclaimers including intellectual property?

2.9 Does the App appropriately disclose the presence of any advertising or sponsorship relationships with vested interests either prior to download or upon first use of the App, with the nature and source of any advertising clearly distinguished from other App content?

Domain 3: Usability & Equity

3.1 For web Apps: does the App claim, or appear to support international Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 level AA compliance (or later)?

3.2 Was the App designed and tested with intended target users?

3.3 Was the App designed with the needs of people with low digital literacy, or a disability access needs in mind?

3.4 Does the App, or associated website, clearly provide information and instructions for use of the App and are the instructions provided in the app clear and in language that is easy to understand for the target user, and does the App alert the user and/or healthcare professional if any inputs or outputs are out of accepted normal ranges or mitigate against other foreseeable use errors?

Domain 4: Privacy & Security

4.1 If the App requires entry of personal information and health data, does it comply with all relevant Australian Commonwealth and Jurisdictional privacy policies including storage and location of health data?

4.2 Does the App provide clear information on how user-derived data is used by the App and in any algorithms, decision-making processes, or automated actions that may affect the health of the user (whether that data is derived by devices or direct user entry)?

4.3 Does the App collect personal data (whether from user entry or via connected devices), and if so does the App provide users with:1.Easy access to their personal data, including the ability to view, download, and understand the data collected about them. 2. The ability to easily and permanently erase their data from the Appâ€™s records? 3. The ability to easily withdraw or withhold consent for the collection, storage, or distribution of their data, whether there is an option for users to opt out from sharing their data, and whether users can access, copy, amend, or request deletion of their data.

4.4 Does the App obtain explicit consent from users before collecting their personal data and using beyond its stated primary intent including use of such data for AI training purposes?

4.5 Does the App, or associated website: 1. Clearly disclose third parties who may obtain or use any personal information provided (including for training AI systems); and, 2. Show how third parties may use the data and how that usage may be terminated by the user if desired; and 3. Disclose any data retention or destruction process?

4.6 Does the App purport to adhere to the following: 1. Contemporary data storage, data exchange and messaging standards that would be regarded as appropriately secure; and 2. Strong encryption methods which are used to protect data in transit and at rest; and 3. Conformance with standards for accessing national or other major health records and health services.

4.7 If required, does the App implement secure authentication methods, such as multi-factor authentication or use of relevant ID codes, or biometrics to verify user identity (clinical or non-clinical as required) to authenticate who is using the App and that they are appropriately authorised?

Domain 5: Technical & Quality

5.1 Is the App in an operationally supported state, beyond beta or development versions? (if part of a research project, then the App may not be operationalised in which case evidence of such research usage may be asked to be provided).

5.2 Is the App technically reliable during usage and is it usable on a wide range of platforms (not just the most recent top-end mobile device)?

5.3 Does the App use AI or machine learning, and if so, has it been subjected to examination for biases or training errors?

5.4 If the App uses AI or machine learning, is it compliant with relevant consumer laws and has it been reviewed by expected user communities with respect to unhealthy or unethical behaviours (expected or unexpected)

Is the App available across different devices, operating systems, and screen sizes for the expected user community?
